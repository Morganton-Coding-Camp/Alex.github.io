Funeral set for ETX 13-year-old boy killed after bus collided with train

(Source: Hannigan Smith Funeral Home Home)
Posted at 6:20 PM, Jan 25, 2019
and last updated 2019-01-28 17:05:45-05

The funeral for an East Texas middle school student who died when his school bus collided with a train will be held on Friday. 

According to Athens ISD, 13-year-old Christopher Bonilla died after a school bus collided with a train on Jan. 25. Another student, 9-year-old Joselyne Torres, is now out of the ICU and listed in stable condition, according to WFAA. 

Joselyn was in "critically stable condition" at Children's Medical Center in Dallas since Friday.

Athens ISD has set up a fund for both Christopher and Joselyn at First State Bank. Deposits can be made at the following locations: downtown Athens, Tyler Street, Malakoff, Mabank, Gun Barrel City and Corsicana.  

The funeral for Christopher is set to be held on Friday at 11 a.m. at the Athens High School gymnasium. There will be no school at any Athens ISD campus on Friday.

Any athletic event that was previously scheduled to be held at AHS on Friday is set to be rescheduled. 

In honor of Christopher's memory, multiple school districts wore maroon on Monday.

The Athens ISD family has been overwhelmed by the outpouring of support in the wake of Fridayâ€™s tragedy. Across the region, the call has gone out: Maroon on Monday. Throughout the city, the county and East Texas, join with us in wearing a maroon or Hornet spirit shirt Monday as a visible sign of your love and support. EDIT: we are absolutely overwhelmed by the outpouring of people saying they will be wearing maroon on Monday. We would love to get pictures from across Texas that we can share. Please message them to us. So much love to you all.

On Friday, an Athens ISD bus collided with a Union Pacific train around 4 p.m., according to a Union Pacific spokesman.

The bus driver, 78-year-old John Stevens, was taken to the hospital by ambulance. Officials said the driver was hired a year ago.

The bus was heading southbound and the train was going westbound, according to officials. They said the boy and the driver were found outside of the bus, the girl had to be extracted from the bus.

No other students were on the bus when the crash happened, according to the school district.

According to officials, the investigation for the crash is still on-going.

Athens ISD released a statement on the incident:

It is with great sorrow that we confirm one of our precious middle school students lost his life today when a district school bus collided with a train in Athens. One other student on the bus, a female at Central Athens Elementary, was care flighted to Children's in Dallas. The bus driver was transported by ambulance. No other students were on the bus. We ask that you join with us in humbly praying for their families.

They also said out of respect, all athletic events scheduled at AISD have been canceled Friday evening.
